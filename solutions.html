Individual

According to Imperial College London and United Nations

  1. Eat less meat and dairy
    • Eat fewer or smaller portions of meat, especially beef and lamb
    • reduce dairy products or switch them for non-dairy alternatives
    • Try to choose fresh, seasonal produce that is grown locally
    • Consider eating insects

  2. Eat more vegetables
    • Eating more vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds, and less meat and dairy
    • Consider vegeterian or vegan diet

  3. Throw away less food
    • purchase only what you need, use what you buy and compost any leftovers

  4. Cut back on flying
    • When you can, meet virtually, take a train, or skip that long-distance trip altogether
  5. Use public transport.walk or bike instead of cars
    • Walking or riding a bike instead of driving will reduce greenhouse gas emissions
    • For longer distances, consider taking a train or bus
  6. Switch to an electric vehicle
    • In many countries, electric cars help reduce air pollution and cause significantly fewer greenhouse gas emissions than gas or diesel-powered vehicles.

  7. Change your home's source of energy to renewable souces like wind or solar
    • install solar panels on your roof to generate energy for your home

  8. Reduce, reuse, repair and recycle
    • To protect the climate, buy fewer things, shop second-hand, and repair what you can

  9. Reduce your energy use, and bills
    • Avoid polluting wood burners and learn about more energy efficient ways to cook – including putting a lid on pans, and using a slow cooker, air fryer or microwave as opposed to the oven or a barbecue.
    • Make simple changes to how you use hot water, like buying a water-efficient shower head, only filling the kettle with as much water as you need, and using the washing machine at 30° only when full.

  10. Cut consumption and waste
    • Avoid single-use items.
    • Minimise food waste by planning meals and properly storing leftovers, and consider composting organic waste.
    • Sort your waste into the right categories so it can be recycled properly

  11. Spread awareness by talking about the changes you make
    • share your experience with your family, friends, customers and clients. Talk positively, and be honest about the ups and downs.

Singapore government sets up various councils and boards to promote climate change, driving change as a country.

  1. Singapore Environment Council

    2. According to its official website, The Singapore Environment Council (SEC) was established in 1995 as an independently managed, non-profit, non-governmental organisation (NGO). We are the only recognised member of the Global Ecolabelling Network (GEN) in Singapore. The GEN is a non-profit association of leading eco labelling organisation worldwide. In 2018, SEC was granted United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) accredited environmental NGO status. We influence thinking on sustainability issues and coordinate environmental efforts in Singapore and the region. We are an approved Institution of Public Character (IPC) with tax exemption status to donors.

    It promotes climate change using these actions:

    • Eco – Certifications
    • Training and Education, which includes Experiential Trails & Workshops and Enterprise Sustainability Course
    • Environmental Awards to companies and schools
    • The Singapore Green Labelling Scheme (SGLS), for Pulp and Paper Products and industrial and consumer products
    • GreenDNA - certification system by the Singapore Environment Council (SEC)

Communities in Singapore do help to promote climate change too.

According to towrds Zero Waste SG, these are some communities.

  1. Zero Waste SG

    2. Zero Waste SG is a not-for-profit and non-governmental organisation dedicated to help Singapore eliminate the concept of waste, and accelerate the shift towards zero waste and the circular economy. It started as a website in 2008 providing tips and resources on waste minimisation and recycling, and is officially registered as a non-governmental organisation in 2015.

    Some activities done by Zero Waste includes:

    • Zero Waste. It is a structured schools programme was developed by Zero Waste SG to empower stakeholders within the school to care inclusively and practise values-driven environmental education (EE).
    • Let's recycle together. It is a programme that impacts different groups of people, by campaigns, posters and community programmes.
    • Bring Your Own (BYO) Singapore movement. It is a movement where 430 retail outlets offered incentives to customers who bring their own reusable bags, bottles or containers.
  2. Green Nudge

    3. Green Nudge is an initiative set up to tackle the problem of contamination of recyclables, and large amount of waste generated at mass and sporting events. It aims to change the narrative of recycling in Singapore by working with eco-conscious companies and community partners to create a decisive and grounded impact to Singapore’s environmental landscape.

    It promotes climate change using these actions:

    • Sustained Engagements - which is done through Cleanups, Workshops, Talks and Sharings, and Trails.
    • Sponsored Engagements - help organisations identify causes that align with their business objectives as well as Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) goals through meaningful community engagements. This collective approach not only promotes shared responsibility but also fosters a mutually beneficial relationship between organisations and the communities they serve.
    • Training - The Green Ambassador, by Advocating, Championing and being a Changemaker.
    • Consultancy - Sustainable Event Management, Waste and Carbon Footprint Audits and Sustainability Branding.
